For decades, we relied on Moore's Law, but classical silicon chips are hitting physical limits. We are entering a new era where we solve the world's hardest problems not by just computing faster, but by computing differently.

Bits vs. Qubits: The Exponential Leap

Classical computers use bits (0 or 1), processing tasks sequentially like a single flashlight scanning a room. Quantum computers use qubits. Thanks to superposition, they can represent multiple states at once.
